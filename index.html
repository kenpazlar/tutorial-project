How to make Kombucha

Kombucha is a flavored bacterial tea with many health benefits, and a variety of flavors.

It's reletively easy to make at home, but realize its going to take some time to ferment.

In the end costing much less than a storebought product and individualizing it to your particular taste.

Ingredients needed:

  • 1) Black or green tea. Do not use flavored tea, it ruins the fermentation process.
  • 2) SCOBY (symbiotic culture of bacteria and yeast) mother culture, this is a live yeast bacteria culture and 1/2 cup of already brewed kombucha.
  • 3) One or two cups of organic cane sugar
  • 4) 4 large quart bottles with airtight stoppers and 1 large clean metal pot.
  • 5) Cheesecloth and rubber bands.
  • 6) Flavorings(ginger to rosemary, blueberry to lemon), personal choice.

    Getting started:

  • 1) Bring 4 quarts of water to a boil in pot. Let boil at least 1 minute.
  • 2)Remove pot from water add 1 to 2 cups organic cane sugar, dissolve thoughrally.
  • 3) Let cool down to 88 degress. Any warmer it will kill the SCOBY. Add the SCOBY and 1/2 cup kombucha.
  • 4) Pour into large glass jar and cover with cheesecloth, secure with rubber band. it must be able to breathe.
  • 5) Place jar in dark warm place to begin fermentation. This will take anywhere from 7 days to 20 days. The less time spent fermenting the sweeter the tea.
  • 6) After a few days a new SCOBY will begin to appear. It looks ugly, but as long as it doesnt turn black or smell bad, it's ok.
  • 7) Once your tea is fermented to where you want it, it's time to flavor it. Remove the SCOBY. Pour the tea into the 4 quart bottles.
  • 8) Add to the tea fruit juice, pulverized fresh fruit, herbs or roots. Or any flavors you want.
  • The flavored tea must now be placed back in dark warm place for three more days, for a second fermentation. The bottles must be sealed air tight during this time and will become fizzy.

    • Drink and enjoy!
